# Fact Checking

Use this file when an article contains factual claims that may be time-sensitive, source-dependent, quantitative, legal, financial, regulatory, technical-standard-related, or product-specific.

## When Verification Is Required

Verify before writing or editing claims about:

- Current policies, laws, regulations, exchange rules, and broker rules.
- Market prices, interest rates, exchange rates, index levels, valuation data, fund flows, and company financials.
- Company executives, product releases, roadmaps, specs, benchmarks, and availability.
- Technical standards, protocol versions, documentation, product manuals, and official specifications.
- Recent events, news, announcements, clarifications, or corrections.

## Source Preference

Prefer primary and authoritative sources:

1. Official laws, policies, standards, specifications, and regulator documents.
2. Company filings, announcements, official documentation, product briefs, and manuals.
3. Standards bodies, exchanges, central banks, statistical agencies, and official datasets.
4. Academic papers, white papers, conference material, and engineering presentations.
5. Reputable media, analyst reports, and high-quality secondary sources.

Avoid low-quality reposts when primary sources are available.

## Date Discipline

- Use concrete dates for time-sensitive facts.
- Prefer `截至 YYYY-MM-DD` when describing current status.
- Do not rely on vague words such as "recently", "currently", "now", or "latest" without verifying and anchoring them to a date.
- If sources conflict, state the conflict and avoid pretending certainty.

## Claim Discipline

- Do not invent sources, quotes, dates, numbers, policy language, specs, or market prices.
- Separate confirmed facts from interpretation and possible scenarios.
- If a claim cannot be verified, remove it, weaken it, or mark it as uncertain.
- Use links in the final `参考资料` chapter according to `references/common.md`.
